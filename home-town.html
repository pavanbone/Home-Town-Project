About My Home Town & Places :-

Anantapur, officially known as Anantapuramu, is a prominent city in the Anantapur district of Andhra Pradesh, India. Situated in the Rayalaseema region, it lies approximately 210 km north of Bengaluru and 148 km south of Kurnool .

Historical Significance :-

boasts a rich historical heritage, having been part of the Vijayanagara Empire. The city was known as Hande Anantapuram during this period. Over the centuries, it came under the rule of various dynasties, including the Pallavas, Chalukyas, and later the Qutub Shahis and the Mughals.

Geography and Climate :-

Located at an elevation of 335 meters, Anantapur experiences a semi-arid climate. Summers are hot and dry, with temperatures often exceeding 37Â°C. The region receives limited rainfall, averaging around 560 mm annually, making it one of the driest areas in Andhra Pradesh.

Demographics:-

per the 2011 census, Anantapur city had a population of 340,613. The literacy rate stands at 82%, with Telugu being the official and widely spoken language. Other languages like Urdu, Hindi, Kannada, and Tamil are also spoken by minority communities.

Economy and Infrastructure :-

serves as a significant commercial and administrative center in the region. The city's GDP was estimated at $1.728 billion. It is well-connected by National Highways 44 and 205, linking it to major cities like Bengaluru, Hyderabad, and Chennai. The Anantapur railway station is classified as an A-category station under the South Central Railway zone.

Cultural and Educational Hub :-

city is home to several educational institutions, including Sri Krishnadevaraya University and the Jawaharlal Nehru Technological University. Culturally, Anantapur is known for its traditional cuisine, which prominently features millets like jowar, bajra, and ragi, as well as peanuts used in various dishes.

Places of Interest :-

Anantapur offers several attractions for visitors:

    Gooty Fort: A historic fort with origins dating back to the Vijayanagara Empire.
    ISKCON Temple: A spiritual center attracting devotees and tourists alike.
    Clock Tower: A prominent landmark built to commemorate India's independence.
    Sri Mounagiri Kshetram: Known for its 11-meter tall monolithic Hanuman statue.
